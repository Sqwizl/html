TURBOS

A forced induction device that increases an internal combustion engine's power output by forcing extra air into the combustion chamber. While all turbos function similarly, there are different types, such as single, twin, and variable geometry turbos, each with its own characteristics and applications.

Turbos are distinguished from superchargers in that a turbo is powered by the kinetic energy of the exhaust gases, whereas a supercharger is mechanically powered usually by a belt from the engine's crankshaft

COILOVERS

Coilovers are what any car enthusiast will have installed on thier car. Coilovers gives the car adjustable ride height. Two of the main types of coilovers are full coilovers and slip-on coilovers. Full coilovers are matched up with a shock absorber from the factory, while with slip-on coilovers, the dampers and springs are bought separately and then assembled

Adjustable coilovers have variable ride height and preload, adjusted using a simple threaded spring perch similar to a nut.
